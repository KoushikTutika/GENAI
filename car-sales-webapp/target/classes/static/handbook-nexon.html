Common Error Codes & Solutions

Error Code: P0420

Catalyst System Efficiency Below Threshold

Symptoms:

  • Check engine light illuminated on dashboard
  • Reduced fuel efficiency
  • Possible sulfur smell from exhaust
  • Failed emissions test

Resolution Steps:

  1. Initial Diagnosis: Connect OBD-II scanner to confirm error code P0420
  2. Check Oxygen Sensors: Test upstream and downstream O2 sensors (should read 0.1-0.9V fluctuating). Replace if readings are flat or out of range
  3. Inspect Catalytic Converter: Check for physical damage, rattling sounds, or excessive heat discoloration. Use infrared thermometer to check temperature difference (inlet should be hotter than outlet)
  4. Check for Exhaust Leaks: Inspect all exhaust connections, gaskets, and welds for leaks that could affect sensor readings
  5. Replace Components: If catalytic converter is damaged, replace with genuine TATA parts (Part #: varies by year)
  6. Clear Codes: Use OBD-II scanner to clear fault codes
  7. Test Drive: Drive vehicle for 50-100 km to allow ECU to relearn and verify repair

Error Code: P0171

System Too Lean (Bank 1)

Symptoms:

  • Rough idle or engine hesitation
  • Poor acceleration performance
  • Check engine light on
  • Engine may stall at idle

Resolution Steps:

  1. Inspect Air Filter: Remove and check air filter condition. Replace if dirty or clogged (TATA Part #: varies by model year)
  2. Check for Vacuum Leaks: Inspect all vacuum hoses, intake manifold gasket, and throttle body gasket. Use smoke test or carburetor cleaner spray method to locate leaks
  3. Test Fuel Pressure: Connect fuel pressure gauge. Pressure should be 3.5-4.0 bar at idle. If low, check fuel pump, fuel filter, and fuel pressure regulator
  4. Clean MAF Sensor: Remove Mass Air Flow sensor and clean with MAF sensor cleaner spray. Do not touch sensor element with hands or tools
  5. Check PCV Valve: Inspect Positive Crankcase Ventilation valve for proper operation. Replace if stuck or damaged
  6. Inspect Fuel Injectors: Test injector spray pattern and resistance (should be 12-16 ohms). Clean or replace as needed
  7. Reset ECU: Clear fault codes and disconnect battery for 10 minutes to reset adaptive fuel trim
  8. Verify Repair: Monitor fuel trim values with scanner during test drive (should be -10% to +10%)

Error Code: P0335

Crankshaft Position Sensor Circuit Malfunction

Symptoms:

  • Engine cranks but won't start
  • Engine stalls while driving
  • Intermittent starting issues
  • Tachometer not working or erratic

Resolution Steps:

  1. Locate Sensor: Crankshaft position sensor is located near the crankshaft pulley on the engine block
  2. Visual Inspection: Check sensor wiring harness for damage, corrosion, or loose connections
  3. Clean Sensor Area: Remove sensor and clean mounting surface and sensor tip from metal debris and oil
  4. Test Sensor Resistance: Use multimeter to check sensor resistance (should be 200-300 ohms at room temperature)
  5. Check Air Gap: Verify proper air gap between sensor and reluctor wheel (0.5-1.5mm). Adjust if necessary
  6. Replace Sensor: If faulty, install new crankshaft position sensor (TATA Part #: varies by engine type)
  7. Clear Codes: Use diagnostic tool to clear fault codes
  8. Test Start: Start engine and verify smooth operation and proper tachometer function

Preventive Maintenance Tips

  • Follow TATA recommended service intervals (every 10,000 km or 12 months)
  • Use genuine TATA parts and recommended fluids
  • Keep engine oil at proper level and change as per schedule
  • Replace air filter every 15,000 km or annually
  • Check and clean throttle body every 30,000 km
  • Inspect all hoses and belts during regular service
