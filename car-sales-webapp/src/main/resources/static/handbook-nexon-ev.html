EV-Specific Error Codes & Solutions

Error Code: EV001

Battery Not Charging

Symptoms:

  • Charging indicator not lighting up
  • No charging current detected
  • Charging port LED not active
  • Dashboard shows "Charging Error"

Resolution Steps:

  1. Verify Cable Connection: Ensure charging cable is firmly connected to both vehicle and charging station. Listen for locking click sound
  2. Check Charging Port: Inspect charging port for debris, water, or physical damage. Clean with dry cloth if needed
  3. Test Charging Station: Try charging another EV at same station to verify station is working properly
  4. Check Vehicle Settings: Verify charging is not scheduled for later time in vehicle settings
  5. Inspect Onboard Charger Fuse: Check 40A fuse in fuse box (located under hood). Replace if blown
  6. Reset Charging System: Disconnect cable, turn off vehicle completely, wait 2 minutes, then retry charging
  7. Check BMS Status: Use diagnostic tool to check Battery Management System for fault codes
  8. Contact Service Center: If issue persists, schedule appointment for onboard charger diagnostics

Estimated Cost: ₹0 - ₹25,000 (depending on component)

Time Required: 15 minutes - 3 hours

Error Code: EV015

Reduced Power Mode / Turtle Mode

Symptoms:

  • Significant reduction in acceleration
  • Maximum speed limited to 40-50 km/h
  • Turtle icon displayed on dashboard
  • Power output restricted

Resolution Steps:

  1. Check Battery Temperature: View battery temperature on dashboard (optimal: 20-35°C). If overheated (>45°C), park in shade and allow cooling for 30-60 minutes
  2. Verify State of Charge: Check battery SOC. If below 10%, charge immediately. Reduced power activates to preserve remaining range
  3. Inspect Cooling System: Ensure battery cooling system is functioning. Check coolant level in battery cooling reservoir
  4. Check for Overload: Avoid aggressive acceleration and high-speed driving in extreme temperatures
  5. Perform Battery Calibration: Charge battery to 100%, drive until 10%, then charge back to 100% without interruption
  6. Check Cell Balance: Use diagnostic tool to verify all battery cells are balanced (voltage difference <50mV)
  7. Update Software: Visit service center to update BMS and motor controller software to latest version
  8. BMS Diagnostics: If issue persists, comprehensive BMS diagnostics required at authorized service center

Estimated Cost: ₹0 - ₹15,000 (if components need replacement)

Time Required: 30 minutes - 4 hours

Error Code: EV012

Battery Cell Imbalance

Symptoms:

  • Reduced driving range
  • Inconsistent battery percentage readings
  • Warning message on dashboard
  • Charging stops before reaching 100%

Resolution Steps:

  1. Perform Full Charge Cycle: Charge battery from current level to 100% without interruption
  2. Allow Balancing Time: After reaching 100%, keep vehicle plugged in for additional 2 hours. BMS will automatically balance cells
  3. Deep Discharge (if safe): Drive vehicle until battery reaches 5-10% SOC, then immediately charge to 100%
  4. Repeat Cycle: Perform 2-3 full charge/discharge cycles over a week
  5. Avoid Partial Charging: For next few weeks, try to charge from low SOC to high SOC rather than frequent top-ups
  6. Check Cell Voltages: Use diagnostic tool to monitor individual cell voltages (should be within 50mV of each other)
  7. BMS Recalibration: If imbalance persists, visit service center for BMS recalibration
  8. Cell Replacement: In rare cases, faulty cells may need replacement (covered under 8-year warranty)

Estimated Cost: ₹0 (usually resolved by balancing) - Warranty covers cell replacement

Time Required: 1-2 weeks of cycling

Charging Best Practices

  • Maintain battery charge between 20-80% for daily use
  • Charge to 100% only before long trips
  • Avoid frequent DC fast charging (use AC charging for daily needs)
  • Don't leave battery at 0% or 100% for extended periods
  • Park in shade during hot weather to prevent battery overheating
  • Use scheduled charging during cooler night hours
